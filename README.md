# Kabir Retail Solutions: Regional Sales Performance Audit (2010-2012)

## 1. Executive Summary
Based on the data analysis conducted for the 2010-2012 period, the business is currently experiencing a **declining trend** in sales volume, with total quantity sold dropping from 476 in 2010 to 368 in 2012. Despite this decrease in units, the overall business health remains stable due to consistent activity from the sales team and a total volume of **1,224 units** moved over three years.

## 3. Business Recommendations

### Efficiency
* **Which Sales Rep has the highest transaction count but not the highest revenue?**
  **Abdul** has a high transaction count (6), which is very close to the lead, but generates significantly less revenue ($60,940) than Tola ($82,720).
* **What does this suggest about their sales behavior?**
  This suggests that Abdul focuses on high-frequency, low-value transactions. While he is active and consistent in closing deals, he may need training on upselling or targeting higher-value clients to increase his financial contribution to the company.

### Trend Analysis
* **Should the company hire more staff in 2013 or reduce?**
  The company should **maintain current levels or consider a slight reduction**, but certainly **not hire more staff** at this time.
* **Reasoning:**
  The total quantity sold has seen a steady decline for two consecutive years (from 476 to 380, then to 368). Hiring more staff during a period of declining volume would increase operational costs without a guaranteed return. The focus for 2013 should be on improving the efficiency of the current team and increasing the average transaction value.
